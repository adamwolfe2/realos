## One thing

LeaseStack runs a rental property's online leasing marketing and proves what it produces: every ad dollar, website visitor, lead, and tour traced to the signed lease, in one dashboard the operator actually logs into. The word is **proof**, not "intelligence" or "platform."

## Users

Rental-property operators (multifamily, student housing; 1-50 buildings) who market their own vacancies, spend $2-5K/mo across 4+ vendors, and report upward to owners who ask "what are we paying for?" Reference customer: Norman Gensinger, SG Real Estate / Telegraph Commons (Berkeley student housing, live tenant).

## Validated problems (ranked, from a real paying operator)

1. "All zeros downstream" — reports show leads/traffic, then go dark at tours → applications → leases
2. Fragmented expensive stack: $3K/mo, 4+ vendors, $214 CPL, spreadsheet reporting
3. ILS sources (Zillow/Apartments.com) and walk-ins invisible to any system
4. No defensible owner-ready report; wants first-touch attribution, AM monthly / PM daily cadence

## Tone

Operator-credible, direct, quietly confident. Outcome-first, specific over general. Operator-to-operator. Every claim names a concrete thing the product does. No em dashes. No named competitors. Never claim to replace leasing staff. LeaseStack tracks ad performance; it does NOT manage ads or produce creative (agency-strip rule).

## Anti-references

- Category language: "leasing intelligence," "data intelligence," "unified platform," "tech stack," "seamless," "AI-powered," "take control," "digital infrastructure"
- Vendor-swap framing ("replace your marketing stack") — Norman pushed back; we're infrastructure on top of what they use
- Claims without product surface: Keyword Trends, SFR/BTR vertical, AEO as standalone
- Marketing math; use real production numbers only (3,007 residents synced, 111 active leases, live in 14 days)

## Strategic principles

- One live tenant = the proof asset. Real numbers beat invented social proof.
- Month-to-month, you own your data, keep the site if you leave — the risk-reversal is central to the offer.
- Pilot motion: free, on a real property, "we show you what we see, then you decide."
