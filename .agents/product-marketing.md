## The one thing (read this first)

**LeaseStack runs a property's online leasing marketing and puts every result —
ad dollar, website visitor, lead, tour, signed lease — in one dashboard the
operator actually logs into.**

The old site failed because it described a *category* ("leasing intelligence,"
"data intelligence platform," "centralize your tech stack") instead of *what
happens*. Nobody wakes up wanting a "unified platform." They wake up not knowing
which ad produced last month's four leases, staring at six vendor logins, with
an owner asking what they're paying for. That's what we sell against.

**Ban list — these phrases are fired unless immediately followed by a concrete thing the product does:**
"leasing intelligence" · "data intelligence" · "signals" · "AI-powered" ·

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** The dashboard that runs a rental property's online leasing and
shows which marketing actually produced signed leases.

**What it does:** LeaseStack builds and runs a property's leasing website, ad
campaigns (Google, Meta), an after-hours AI chatbot that books tours, a visitor
pixel that puts names and emails on anonymous website traffic, and review
monitoring — then ties all of it to one dashboard that shows the funnel from
website visitor → lead → tour → application → signed lease, and which source
produced each. An AI briefing lands in the operator's inbox every Monday with
three specific actions. Built and managed by the LeaseStack team; live in 14 days.

**Product category (what shelf we're on):** Managed leasing-marketing software
for rental-property operators. Buyers currently search for/compare against
"apartment marketing agency," "multifamily marketing software," "property
management marketing," and their pile of point tools (a website vendor, a Google
Ads freelancer, a chatbot, a reviews tool, a spreadsheet).

**Product type:** Managed SaaS (software + done-for-you agency execution).

**Business model:** $99 per property / month base platform, plus only the
add-on features each property turns on (à la carte, per property / month).
Month-to-month after launch. 14-day free trial, no card. Annual prepay saves 17%.
20+ properties → custom volume pricing.

## Target Audience

**Target companies:** Rental-property operators and owner-operators running
roughly **1–50 buildings** who market their own vacancies online. Sweet spot:
**student housing, multifamily, and senior living**; also commercial. They own or
manage the physical asset and are directly on the hook for occupancy and lease-up
pace. Typically a lean team — the same handful of people juggle leasing, ops, and
"marketing."

**Decision-makers:**
- **Owner / principal / managing partner** — cares about occupancy, lease-up
  speed, and whether marketing spend is producing leases. Signs the check.
- **Operations / leasing director** — lives in the day-to-day; feels the pain of
  scattered tools and after-hours leads going cold.
- **Marketing lead (if any)** — often a part-timer or an agency retainer they're
  unhappy with.

In a 1–50 building operator these often collapse into one or two people. Write to
the owner-operator who does all three jobs.

**Primary use case:** "I'm spending on ads and running a website, but I can't
tell what's working, leads slip through overnight, and I can't answer my owners
when they ask what the marketing is doing."

**Jobs to be done:**
- *Show me which marketing dollar produced which signed lease* — so I stop
  guessing and can defend the budget.
- *Stop losing leads after hours* — capture and book tours at 2am without hiring
  a night shift.
- *Give me one place to look* instead of six vendor dashboards and a spreadsheet.

**Use cases / scenarios:**
- A student-housing lease-up racing an August move-in deadline (this is the
  flagship — Telegraph Commons in Berkeley).
- An owner-operator who fired their agency and is now drowning in Google Ads,
  Meta, and a website vendor with no unified reporting.
- A small multifamily portfolio where the principal wants a Monday report that
  tells the team what to do this week, not a quarterly deck of impressions.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Operators spend real money marketing their vacancies online but
have no honest, single view of what that money produces — and leads leak out of
the funnel (overnight, anonymous, unanswered) while they're stuck reconciling six
vendor dashboards.

**The problems, ranked, in operator language:**

1. **"I don't know which ad dollar produced which lease."** Google Ads shows
   clicks, Meta shows reach, the website vendor shows traffic — none of them tie
   to a signed lease. The operator is flying blind on the biggest lever they
   control. *(LeaseStack: one funnel — visitors → leads → tours → applications →
   signed leases — with each lead's true source.)*

2. **"Leads fall through overnight."** A prospect asks "is the 2-bed still
   available?" at 2am and nobody answers until morning — by then they've toured a
   competitor. Roughly **1 in 4 leads arrive after hours.** *(LeaseStack: an AI
   chatbot trained on the property's real units answers and books the tour at 2am.)*

3. **"Most of my website traffic is anonymous and wasted."** Hundreds of people
   visit the site, look at floor plans, and leave without filling out a form.
   *(LeaseStack: a visitor pixel puts a name, email, and intent on the traffic the
   forms missed — the demo property identifies ~312/month, ~41% of traffic named.)*

4. **"My owners ask what they're paying me for, and I don't have a clean answer."**
   The reporting is a quarterly deck of impressions and clicks, or a spreadsheet
   stitched together by hand. *(LeaseStack: a Monday-morning AI briefing with 3
   specific actions, plus client-ready reports that generate themselves.)*

5. **"Reviews and mentions pile up unanswered."** Google, Yelp, Reddit, and the
   open web are talking about the property and nobody's watching. *(LeaseStack:
   every public mention in one inbox, sentiment-tagged, one-click reply.)*

6. **"We're invisible when renters ask AI."** Prospects increasingly ask ChatGPT,
   Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini "best student housing near Berkeley" — and the
   property isn't in the answer. *(LeaseStack: neighborhood pages built to rank
   and get cited, plus tracking of when AI search actually quotes the site.)*

**Why current alternatives fall short:**
- **A marketing agency retainer:** expensive, opaque, grades its own homework,
  and hands over decks — not a live dashboard the operator owns. No after-hours
  capture, no visitor identification.
- **A pile of point tools (website vendor + Google Ads freelancer + chatbot +
  reviews tool + spreadsheet):** six invoices, six logins, and nothing ties ad
  spend to signed leases. The spreadsheet is the "integration."
- **Their PMS (AppFolio etc.):** manages units, leases, and accounting — it does
  not market vacancies, capture demand, or attribute leads. Different job.

**What it costs them:** Wasted ad spend on channels they can't measure. Leads lost
to faster competitors every single week. Hours of manual reporting. And the
slow-burn cost of an owner losing confidence because nobody can prove the
marketing works.

**Emotional tension:** The nagging feeling of paying for something you can't see
working. The dread of the owner's "so what's this actually doing for us?"
question. The low-grade guilt of knowing leads are slipping and not having the
bandwidth to catch them.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct — apartment/multifamily marketing agencies & marketing-software point
tools:** Deliver channels but not a single owned dashboard tying spend to signed
leases; retainers are opaque and self-graded; no visitor identification or
after-hours capture; you rent the results, you don't own the data.

**Secondary — DIY stack (website builder + Google/Meta self-serve + a chatbot +
a reviews tool + spreadsheet):** Technically covers the surface area, but the
operator becomes the systems integrator. No cross-channel attribution, no
after-hours booking, no identity resolution, and it eats the exact hours a lean
team doesn't have.

**Indirect — "do nothing / rely on the PMS + listing sites":** The PMS runs
leases and accounting; ILS listing sites (Apartments.com, Zillow, etc.) rent
eyeballs but hand back no dashboard, no pixel, no attribution, and no ownership.
Doing nothing means continuing to fly blind.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators (things the alternatives don't do):**
- **Ad-spend → signed-lease attribution in one funnel.** Not clicks. Leases.
- **After-hours AI chatbot that books tours** off the property's real unit data.
- **Visitor pixel that names anonymous website traffic** (name, email, intent).
- **AI-search visibility** — pages built to get cited, plus tracking of when
  ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity/Gemini actually quote the site.
- **You own everything.** The domain stays yours the whole time; on exit you get
  a static export of the site plus your full lead history — no transition fee.
- **Built and live in 14 days**, then month-to-month. Not a 12-month agency lock-in.
- **One dashboard, à-la-carte features** — pay for the base platform plus only
  what each property turns on.

**How we do it differently:** LeaseStack is software the operator logs into,
run by a team that does the execution. It's not "hire an agency" (opaque, rented)
and not "buy six tools" (you integrate them). It's one system that does the work
*and* shows its work.

**Why that's better:** The operator finally sees what marketing produces, stops
losing overnight leads, and can answer their owners with a number instead of a
shrug — without hiring, without a year-long contract, without owning the plumbing.

**Why customers choose us:** They can try it on *their own property with their own
data* before paying, they're never locked in, and they keep everything if they
leave. The risk of trying is close to zero; the cost of not trying compounds every
week.

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Operator-to-operator. Direct, quietly confident, outcome-first. Sounds
like a sharp GM who's run a lease-up, not a SaaS brochure.

**Style rules:**
- **Plain operator English.** If a leasing director wouldn't say it out loud,
  cut it.
- **Short sentences.** One idea each. Read it aloud; if you run out of breath,
  split it.
- **Specific over general** — but **numbers only when true.** Use the real
  demo/product figures (1 in 4 leads after hours; ~312 visitors identified/month;
  14 days to live; $99 base). Never invent a stat.
- **Name the concrete thing** the product does; never hide behind category words.
- **Lead with the problem or the outcome,** not the feature name.
- No emojis (use Lucide icons in UI). No em dashes as decoration. No named
  competitors. Never claim to replace the leasing team.

**Personality (3–5 adjectives):** Straight-talking · credible · efficient ·
confident · low-hype.
