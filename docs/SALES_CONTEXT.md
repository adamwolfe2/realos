## 3. What's broken today

This is the picture every prospect recognizes. Use it to anchor the conversation before showing the product.

**Six vendors, six invoices.** Property management lives in AppFolio. Marketing lives in Conversion Logix or a local agency. The website lives on Wix or WordPress from 2018. The chatbot is a separate vendor or doesn't exist. Listings are manually copy-pasted to listing portals. Lead nurture lives in a sales rep's inbox. Reporting lives in a quarterly PDF that says "impressions are up."

**Activity, not outcomes.** Agency decks show impressions, clicks, and reach. They do not show leases. The link between marketing dollars and signed contracts is broken, so nobody can defend or cut the spend with confidence.

**The site is a brochure.** Most operator sites do not capture identity, do not surface live availability, do not have a useful chatbot, and do not retarget visitors. The traffic shows up, looks around, and disappears.

**The chatbot is a glorified FAQ.** It answers one canned question, forgets the visitor, and captures zero leads after hours. Prospects asking real questions at 2am get a contact form.

**Creative ships in weeks.** A new ad concept means a change-order with the agency, a meeting, and three rounds of revisions. Two weeks later it ships. By then the seasonality has moved.

## 4. The product

LeaseStack is one platform with nine modules, all built and shippable today. Operators pick the modules they want at intake. The platform itself is multi-tenant, so every customer logs into the same product with their own brand, their own listings, and their own playbook.

| Module | What it does |
|---|---|
| Managed marketing site | Custom-built on the operator's domain. Fast, search-friendly, updated by live listing sync. |
| PMS listing sync | Every unit on the site matches the source of truth within the hour. |
| AI chatbot | Trained on the operator's properties. Answers in five seconds. Captures leads around the clock. Hands hot ones to the team with full conversation history. |
| Visitor identification | Names and emails on a meaningful share of anonymous site traffic. |
| Lead capture and CRM | Forms, exit intent, chat, and pixel-identified visitors all flow into one pipeline with automated nurture. |
| Google and Meta ads | Geo-fenced campaigns, retargeting, creative refreshed weekly. |
| Search and AI discovery | Rank in Google. Get recommended by ChatGPT and Perplexity. Per-location pages. |
| Creative studio | On-demand ad creative. 48-hour turnaround. No retainers, no templates. |

Every customer gets the operator portal: dashboards, leads, conversations, ads, campaigns, creative, SEO, properties, reports, and a weekly performance email. Role-based access for owner, asset manager, property manager, leasing agent, and viewer.

Bring-your-own-site mode is supported. If the operator likes their existing site, we install the chatbot, visitor identification, and CRM into it without rebuilding.

## 5. Value propositions, with proof points

Use these in the order that matches the prospect's pain. The first three are the heaviest hitters.

### "Outcomes, not activity."

The weekly report ties marketing spend to signed leases. It is one page, readable over coffee, and shows leases attributed to sources, total spend, anomalies flagged, and recommended tests for the week. Operators have never had this view of their own funnel.

### "One platform replaces six vendors."

The typical replacement: agency, chatbot vendor, ad creative shop, listing portal, CRM, and the part-time consultant stitching them together. LeaseStack collapses all of it into one login, one bill, one team. Most operators consolidate within the first thirty days.

### "Fourteen days from intake to live."

Day 1 is a thirty-minute intake call where we audit the current marketing stack live. Day 7 is a site preview on a staging URL. Day 14 we flip DNS, the pixel starts firing, the chatbot goes live, and ads begin running. There is no phase-two surprise. This timeline is enforced by the product, not the team.

### "AI on the night shift."

The chatbot is trained on the operator's specific properties. It handles the long tail of prospect questions ("can my dog visit", "do you have furnished options for August") with grounded answers, not canned scripts. Hot leads get handed to the leasing team with the full thread attached.

### "Identity on your traffic."

Most operator sites lose the majority of their traffic to anonymity. The visitor pixel surfaces names and emails on a meaningful share of visitors who never filled out a form. Those leads flow directly into the CRM and into a nurture sequence.

### "Creative in 48 hours."

A request for a new ad concept, a landing page block, or an email subject variant ships within 48 hours. No retainer to unlock it, no change-order, no agency revision rounds.

### "You own the data and the domain."

Domain stays on the operator's account throughout. On exit, we hand over a static export of the site plus the full lead history. No hostage data, no transition fee.

### "Fair-housing compliant by default."

Every creative we ship, ads, landing copy, email, runs through a vertical-specific compliance check before it goes live. Student housing, multifamily, and senior living each have their own rules, with audit trails for each.

## 8. Why LeaseStack vs. the alternatives

### vs. an agency

Agencies bill activity. LeaseStack bills a product. An agency rebuilds the website, runs ads, and stitches together vendor data into a quarterly report. LeaseStack is one platform with a single dashboard, weekly reports tied to leases, 48-hour creative turnaround, and no agency middleman. Most prospects are paying $5,000 to $20,000 per month for an agency that delivers less than the LeaseStack baseline.

### vs. a "marketing automation" tool

Tools like HubSpot, Mailchimp, and the like are horizontal. They were not designed for real estate. They have no PMS sync, no fair-housing compliance layer, no chatbot grounded in property facts, no listing pages, and no managed ads. Operators end up paying for the tool plus the agency to actually run it. LeaseStack is purpose-built for the vertical.

### vs. a property management system

PMS vendors (AppFolio, Yardi, RealPage) own the back office. LeaseStack owns the front. PMS does not build websites, does not run ads, does not capture site identity, and does not have a useful chatbot. LeaseStack sits on top of the PMS and consumes its listing data through the API.

### vs. building it in-house

A real estate operator who tries to build this in-house needs at minimum a marketing manager, a developer, a designer, an ad strategist, and an analyst. The fully-loaded cost runs $400,000 to $700,000 per year and the build takes 12 to 18 months. LeaseStack ships in 14 days for a fraction of that, and the platform compounds across every customer.

## 9. Common objections, with answers

### "We already have a marketing agency."

Most operators run us in parallel with their existing agency for the first month, then consolidate once the dashboards make the comparison obvious. We do not require them to fire the agency on day one.

### "We already have a website we like."

Bring-your-own-site mode installs the chatbot and visitor identification into the existing site without rebuilding it. They keep the design, we add the intelligence layer.

### "What if you can't connect to our PMS?"

We support every major PMS through standard APIs and run a nightly backfill for edge cases. If the prospect's PMS is unusual, we confirm on the intake call. We have not yet hit a PMS we couldn't connect to.

### "How long is the contract?"

Month to month after launch. The build fee covers the first 14 days. After that, if we are not moving lease velocity, the operator cancels and the platform stays on their domain through the following cycle.

### "What if we want to leave?"

The operator owns the domain throughout. On exit we hand over a static export of the site plus full lead history. No transition fee, no data hostage, no enterprise services contract.

### "Are you fair-housing compliant?"

Every creative ships through a vertical-specific compliance check (student housing, multifamily, senior living have separate rule sets). Audit trails for every piece of creative are kept inside the operator portal.

### "Who actually does the work? Are you just AI?"

We have a real ops team that builds the site, runs the ads, designs the creative, and writes the playbook. AI does the night-shift conversations, the per-location SEO pages, the visitor scoring, and the creative ideation. Humans approve and ship. The mix is what makes the 14-day timeline possible.

### "What if we need a feature that isn't built yet?"

Most platform requests we hear are already on the roadmap. Specific feature asks from launch customers are weighted heavily in the next sprint. We have shipped operator-requested features inside the same week of launch in multiple cases.

### "How is this different from Conversion Logix / Apartments.com / RentCafe?"

Those products solve one slice. Conversion Logix runs ads. Apartments.com is a listing portal. RentCafe is a PMS-attached website builder. None of them give the operator a unified dashboard that ties spend to leases, an AI chatbot trained on their properties, identity on anonymous traffic, and a 48-hour creative studio in one product. We replace the patchwork.
