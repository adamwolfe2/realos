### A Partnership Proposal for Building the Operating Layer of Modern Real Estate

Prepared for: Norman Gensinger, Director, GSC/Spectrum Partners

## Why We're Writing This

Over the last several weeks we've been building the new Telegraph Commons website, the AI chatbot, and the early scaffolding of a platform we're calling LeaseStack. What started as a website refresh has become something much bigger, and we want to formalize what we think the partnership between LeaseStack and GSC/Spectrum should look like going forward.

The short version: Telegraph Commons is the pilot. GSC/Spectrum is the proving ground. And together we can build the vertical AI operating system that the real estate industry has been waiting for.

---

## The Opportunity

Real estate operators are running a multi-billion dollar industry on a patchwork of disconnected tools. Property management lives in AppFolio. Marketing lives in Conversion Logix or Google Ads. Reporting lives in spreadsheets. Tenant communication lives in email and live chat. Websites live on Wix and WordPress from 2018. Nothing talks to each other, and nobody is applying modern AI to any of it.

Family-run portfolios in the hundred-million to multi-billion dollar range feel this the most. They have the sophistication to know the tools are broken, but not the internal engineering teams to build replacements. They pay thousands of dollars a month for fragmented services that don't move the needle, because there has been no better option.

That's the gap. And it's exactly what LeaseStack is built to close.

---

## The Thesis

**LeaseStack is a vertical AI SaaS platform purpose-built for real estate operators.**

Not a generic marketing tool. Not another CRM. Not a property management system. An operating layer that sits on top of what owners already use, unifies the data, and puts AI to work on the tasks that matter: filling units faster, understanding which marketing spend actually performs, giving asset managers visibility, giving property managers daily direction, and giving tenants and prospects a better experience.

We believe the real estate industry is five years behind the curve on AI adoption, and the first companies to build serious vertical tools for this space will own it.

## Why Norman and GSC/Spectrum

Every startup needs a design partner. Someone who lives inside the customer's shoes, feels the pain, and is willing to work alongside the team to shape the product. Norman is that person, for three reasons.

**He owns the outcome.** As an owner and asset manager, Norman isn't a middle-manager gatekeeper. He can make decisions, he feels the P&L directly, and he knows what success looks like.

His product feedback on role-based views, leasing velocity, monthly AM reports, daily PM actions, referral tracking, and marketing ROI attribution is exactly the blueprint we're building toward.

**GSC/Spectrum is the second customer.** A successful pilot at Telegraph Commons opens the door to the rest of the portfolio, which is a multi-property, multi-asset-class environment that forces the product to mature in the right ways.

---

## Phase 1. Prove It at Telegraph Commons

The first job is simple: make Telegraph Commons a case study nobody in the industry can ignore.

We will capture the current baseline before anything changes. Traffic, lead volume, cost per lead, conversion rates, leasing velocity, occupancy curves, and current marketing spend. These numbers become the "before" in every future conversation we have with prospective customers.

Then we deploy the LeaseStack stack at Telegraph Commons:

- A new high-converting website replacing the current Wix site
- An always-on AI chatbot that qualifies leads and books tours
- A marketing ROI dashboard that shows exactly which channels produce tenants
- A monthly AM report that lands in Norman's inbox automatically
- A daily PM action feed that tells the onsite team what to do today
- An AppFolio data connector that pulls the leasing pipeline into one place

Success is measured against the baseline. Lower cost per lead. Higher lead volume. Faster time from inquiry to signed lease. Lower total marketing spend. Earlier occupancy.

---

## Phase 2. Expand Across the GSC/Spectrum Portfolio

Once Telegraph Commons is working, we roll the product out across the rest of the GSC/Spectrum portfolio. Every property becomes a new data point, a new proof of the model, and a chance to pressure-test LeaseStack in a multi-property, multi-asset-class environment.

This phase is where the product matures from a single-property tool into a true portfolio platform. Role-based dashboards for owners, asset managers, and property managers. Portfolio-wide reporting. Cross-property benchmarking. Centralized marketing attribution across dozens of assets.

By the end of this phase, LeaseStack is no longer a pilot. It's the backbone of how GSC/Spectrum runs its business.

---

## Phase 3. Norman as a Founding Partner

Norman's role evolves as the company does. In the pilot phase he is a customer giving us feedback. In the expansion phase he is a power user stress-testing the platform at scale. In the scale phase he becomes the industry voice that opens doors.

Family-office real estate is a relationship-driven world. Cold outreach doesn't work. Warm introductions from trusted peers do. Norman's network across family-office RE, industry conferences, and peer operators is exactly the distribution engine a vertical SaaS company needs in its early years.

We want Norman positioned as a formal partner in the LeaseStack story. The specific structure is something we'll work out together. What matters here is the principle: he's not just a customer, and we're not just a vendor. We're building something together, and he should share in the upside of what gets built.

---

## Phase 4. Scale to the Broader Market

With a flagship case study, a validated portfolio deployment, and a design partner who can open doors, LeaseStack has the ingredients to scale.

The market is large and underserved. Family-run portfolios. Regional property management companies. Student housing operators. Multifamily owners. Commercial operators. Each segment has slightly different needs, but the core problem is the same: fragmented tools, manual reporting, no AI, and millions of dollars leaking through the cracks.

Our go-to-market leans on three channels. First, the warm network, starting with Norman's introductions to peer operators. Second, industry events where the GSC/Spectrum case study does the selling for us. Third, partnerships with property management companies that want AI capabilities but can't build them internally.

The long-term vision is straightforward. LeaseStack becomes the default operating layer for modern real estate, the way Salesforce became the default for sales and Toast became the default for restaurants.
